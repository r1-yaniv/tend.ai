How to read this deck

We start in mental health.
The primitive is universal.

Specifically: outpatient psychotherapy and psychiatry, where care is delivered through periodic sessions with a single clinician. Every example below is written through that lens.

But the underlying primitive — the half of care that happens between visits — exists in every longitudinal clinician-patient relationship. Throughout the deck we cross-check the design against two other specialties:

CROSS-SPECIALTY · A

Nutrition / dietetics

Prescription is a meal plan. Deviation is an off-plan meal. Deterioration is weight, labs, energy, GI symptoms, or eating-disorder behaviors trending wrong.

CROSS-SPECIALTY · B

Drug adherence

Prescription is a regimen. Deviation is a missed or doubled dose. Deterioration is the underlying condition recurring — and the signal can come from pharmacy data.

Tend The problem space 04 · The Problem
What's wrong with the status quo

Right now, the between-visit half is
invisible. unsupported. unmeasured.

UNSUPPORTED

The patient has no scaffolding.

Partial compliance is the default. Deviation drives deterioration. The treatment plan exists but has nowhere to live between Tuesdays.

INVISIBLE

The clinician has no view.

What actually happened in the gap is not observable. The clinician inherits a curated retrospective at the next session, not a continuous picture.

UNMEASURED

Self-report is all there is.

Recall bias trims the signal before it arrives. Trends that should trigger action don't surface until the patient has been off-track for weeks.

The clinician depends on retrospective self-report and misses an opportunity to act on meaningful, accurate signals that already exist in the patient's life — they just have no path up.

Tend Why it persists 06 · The Problem
The gap isn't open because the field hasn't noticed

It's open because closing it has been
operationally impossible.

CONSTRAINT · 01

Clinicians can't scale themselves.

A typical caseload is 20–40 patients. Per-patient between-session attention beyond a single short message is not viable on standard reimbursement rates.

CONSTRAINT · 02

Existing tools are wrong-shaped.

Self-help apps don't know your treatment plan. Symptom-trackers record but don't intervene. Always-on monitoring crosses a line patients are right to defend.

CONSTRAINT · 03

Privacy is structurally undersolved.

Patients won't be honest if they think the therapist sees everything. Therapists won't engage if they can't trust the data. The field has no widely adopted answer for "how do we share just enough?"

CONSTRAINT · 04

The honest claim is a hard sell.

Effect sizes for general digital MH are small (Linardon 2019; Elmer 2025 d=0.06–0.14 on distress). The honest claim — better adherence, earlier deterioration detection — is harder to market and easier to dismiss.

What's newly tractable: LLMs that can hold a clinician-personalized therapeutic conversation; local-first cryptography that genuinely keeps secrets on-device; a regulatory category (SaMD, prescription DTx) that supports a clinician-prescribed model.

07 · Position
The core vision

We build the infrastructure for the
between-visit half of care.

The clinician writes the plan and shapes the channel. The channel speaks in their voice. The patient's data stays under the patient's control. Inside that frame, Tend helps the patient execute, decides what to escalate, and feeds the right signal back to the clinician — without adding work for the clinician.

Tend The core vision 08 · The Vision
Three principles · everything follows

The frame the system never violates.

PRINCIPLE · 01

The clinician is the lead.

Tend is a co-therapist, not a competing one. Every behavior of the system either executes the clinician's plan, supports the patient inside that plan, or surfaces a signal the clinician asked to see. The system has no independent treatment opinion.

No extra work for the clinician — if it costs them more time than it saves, the platform fails.

PRINCIPLE · 02

The patient owns their data.

Raw conversation, in-the-moment notes, and detailed logs live on the patient's device. What flows up to the clinician is only what the patient has approved. Patient-facing context is only what the clinician explicitly shared down.

A signal floor — at minimum, on-track / off-track always flows up.

PRINCIPLE · 03

Pull-first, with directed push.

No surveillance prompts. No "how are you doing?" pings. Notifications exist only for things the patient and clinician explicitly agreed to be reminded of — an exposure window, a med time, a journal cue.

Empirically validated by Elmer 2025: pull-first beats inferred-distress.

Tend Solution architecture 09 · Architecture
Five core components, one plug-in layer

The same product across specialties.
Only the contents of the boxes change.

01 · CONTRACT
Prescription model
Discrete tasks, continuous behaviors, constraints, check-in cadence, escalation thresholds, AI-assistance level. Authored in the clinician portal.
02 · CHANNEL
Conversational interface
LLM-mediated patient channel. Knows only what the clinician shared. Speaks in a tone the clinician tuned — optionally cloned voice.
03 · TRUST
Local-first & share-up
Conversation and detailed logs live on-device, encrypted. A separate local service extracts shareable summaries the patient explicitly approves.
04 · SIGNAL
Adherence tracking
Objective where possible, self-reported where not. Feeds the patient's progress view and the clinician's adherence signal.
05 · GLANCE
Clinician dashboard
Glance-readable before a session. Adherence, prescription, themes the patient surfaced, escalation events. Pre-session brief, not a new inbox.

On top of these five, a thin per-specialty plug-in tailors the contents: mental health uses exposures, thought records, PHQ-9 / GAD-7. Nutrition uses meal plans and weight / labs. Drug adherence uses regimens and pharmacy data. Same architecture, different boxes.

04 · The signal

Did the prescribed thing
happen — and how?

Objective where possible. Self-reported where not. Two outputs: the patient's progress view and the clinician's adherence signal.

  • Patient-facing. Plan view of prescribed practices; tracking view that shows the rhythm — "mornings landed, evenings drifted."
  • Clinician-facing. A 14-day adherence number, per-practice rhythm, and the patient's own framing of why a slip happened.
  • Engagement, not gamification. No streaks, no badges, no clinical theater. Patients are not the user we optimize against.
  • Cross-specialty: integrate with smart pill bottles / HMO refill data / Apple Health where the signal can be pulled, not asked.

Tend Notes on mental health 16 · MH-specific
Two things that don't transfer cleanly

MH carries weight the architecture
has to explicitly hold.

MH-SPECIFIC · 01

Continuity-as-treatment.

The clinician's voice staying present in the patient's life between visits is itself part of the therapeutic mechanism — working alliance, internalization, intersession process (Orlinsky/Geller).

Outsized design weight on persona, voice, narrative continuity. Possible Delphi-style cooperation for cloned voice.

MH-SPECIFIC · 02

The "is this patient stable enough?" gating decision.

Between-session AI conversation may be wrong for a patient working on tolerating distress without external regulation — or for one with dependency tendencies.

Built into the prescription model from day one — clinician can enable, restrict, or fully disable Maya per patient.

Tend The wedge dilemma 18 · Strategy
Same product · three candidate v1 paths

Which specialty proves the platform fastest?

Mental health is where the gap is most acute and our domain authority is strongest — but provability is the hardest. Two cleaner-signal alternatives are on the table.

Wedge · A · Mental health

Biggest gap.
Hardest to prove.

Strength
Continuity-as-treatment makes the architecture most differentiated. Domain authority is here.
Risk
Effect sizes are small; regulatory bar is high; outcome cycles are long.
Where we're starting — that's the wedge in the brand. Open question on whether it's the wedge for proof.
Wedge · B · Nutrition

Cleaner endpoints.
Faster pilot.

Strength
Weight, labs, photo logs. Behavioral signal is short of pharmacy data but well above MH self-report.
Risk
Continuity story is narrower. Less "co-therapist," more "compliant meal companion."
If we need to be provable to get licensed, this is the path that gets there fastest.
Wedge · C · Drug adherence

Cleanest signal.
Reimbursement story.

Strength
Refill data + smart caps + pharmacy integration. Israeli HMOs are mandatory — clean B2B sale.
Risk
Narrowest conversational story. Closer to a smart pillbox than a co-therapist — under-uses the architecture.
Strongest commercial path; weakest brand fit. A possible v1, a possible v2-after-MH.
